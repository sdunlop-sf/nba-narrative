Block 4 of 4

Measurement Enablement

Measurement is designed in, not bolted on. If the data model doesn't support consistent attribution today, it won't support AI-driven decisioning tomorrow.

Key Questions
1
What does "engagement" mean in your business — opens? Attendance? Script lift? Relationship score?
2
How do you currently attribute field and digital engagement to a measurable outcome? What's the biggest gap?
3
What metrics does leadership report on — and does the data model currently support those metrics?
The data model must support
Consistent event schema — same structure for interactions regardless of source system
Attribution linkage — connect engagement to HCP, campaign, content, and time period
Channel-agnostic metrics — reach, frequency, depth of engagement across field + digital
Segment performance — measure effectiveness by specialty, geography, tier, or journey stage
Future: AI-ready scoring signals — interaction data that can train outcome models

Alignment

Five principles to agree on today.

These aren't aspirational — they're the guardrails we'll test every design decision against. Do we have consensus?

Principle 1
Consistency
The same concept — a customer, an interaction, a content asset — is defined and structured the same way regardless of source system.
Principle 2
Scalability
The model should accommodate new channels, new audience types, and new use cases without requiring a redesign of core entities.
Principle 3
Activation-Ready
Every data entity is designed with an activation use case in mind — segmentation, orchestration, or measurement. Data for its own sake is out of scope.
Principle 4
Privacy First
Consent and preferences are first-class fields in the model, not an afterthought. TGA and Privacy Act compliance is designed in, not retrofitted.
Principle 5
Future-Proof
The model should support AI-driven use cases in Phase 2 — scoring, next-best-action, and outcome prediction — without structural rework.
