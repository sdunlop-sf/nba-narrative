The Petrol Gauge

Six scenarios, each adding a layer of intelligence. Per 1,000 contacts dispatched daily. Every scenario uses DC segments — batch segments trigger arbitration, and action-level eligibility/exclusion segments filter which actions apply per contact. DC is the foundation, not an add-on.

How DC Segments Work in Every Scenario

Trigger: DC batch segments (e.g. Cross_Sell_Prospects) identify which contacts to dispatch. DC RT segments fire instantly on profile changes.
Action eligibility: Each action can require a DC segment (inclusion) or block one (exclusion). Tested: 3/9 Cross-Sell actions gated by Cross_Sell_Prospects segment, 1 excluded by Churn_Risk. Contacts outside the segment see fewer actions (6 instead of 9).

Key Architecture Insight

3. Data Cloud — Activation + Segment Eligibility

DC Segment publish (Run 8A) is the activation event. Three segments published at 10:19:51 UTC with combined ~2,410 members.

Segment membership flows to CRM as NBA_Eligibility__c records. These power two things:

  • Trigger dispatch: NBASegmentTriggerSchedulable reads NBA_Eligibility__c for contacts in the trigger segment, publishes PE events
  • Action eligibility filtering: InvokeArbitration loads contact's segments at line 286, filters actions at lines 546-569. Tested in Track 14: 3/9 actions dropped for contacts outside Cross_Sell_Prospects.

Activation cost scales with segment member count, not action count. 3 segments x ~1,400 members = ~4,200 activation events per publish cycle.

Scaling Notes
  1. CIs scale with data volume, not contact count. If Amaysim has 500k unified individuals in DC (billing + usage + CRM), CI computation will be proportionally larger than our 1,400-individual test.
  2. LLM token estimate is conservative. Tier 1.5 rerank rate (7.5%) was measured with uniform segment-mode scores. With real Einstein scores, the rate may be lower.
  3. CRM storage is the long-term concern. At 8,000 decisions/day = ~2.9M records/year. Recommend 90-day rolling purge of superseded/expired decisions.
  4. Batch dispatch currently limited to batch size 1 due to SOQL governor limits. At 8,000 contacts: ~15-20 min runtime. Bulkification fix scoped for next week (batch size 50+, ~2-3 min).
