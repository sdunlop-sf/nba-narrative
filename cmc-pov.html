A Note First

Thank you for the time and transparency you gave us during the CDP evaluation. The process was well-run, the feedback was direct, and we genuinely appreciated the opportunity to be considered.

We respect the decision. We're not writing to re-open it.

What we did want to share — now that we've had time to reflect on the evaluation properly — is something we don't think we surfaced well enough during the process. Not a feature we missed. A different conversation entirely.

The Conversation We Should Have Had

The CDP evaluation was framed around data routing — how do we move event data from our app to Braze, AppsFlyer, and our analytics stack with minimal engineering lift. On that question, a purpose-built CDP is the right answer, and we understand why the decision went the way it did.

The conversation we didn't have — and wish we had — is what happens after the data arrives. Which customers do you act on, and how do you decide? That's not a CDP question. That's a decisioning question, and it's a different problem with a different solution.

For a trading platform, the decisions that drive revenue are not about data routing. They're about knowing — in real time — which clients are at risk of going dormant, which are ready to increase activity, and which need intervention before it's too late to act.

Today, that logic lives in Braze. Rules, segments, journey conditions — built by the marketing team, manually maintained, and limited by what a rules engine can express. It works. But it doesn't learn, it doesn't adapt, and it doesn't tell you what you don't already know to look for.

The Opportunity

A decisioning layer sits above the CDP and the activation tools. It doesn't replace them — it tells them what to do. Einstein Studio, trained on CMC's own client outcome data, scores every client continuously: likelihood to churn, propensity to increase trading volume, risk of going dormant after a loss event. Those scores flow into whatever activation stack you're running — Braze, direct, in-app — as the signal that determines what fires and when.

The result is not better segments. It's better decisions. The model learns from every outcome — every client who churned after a signal was missed, every intervention that worked — and the next recommendation is better than the last. That's the compounding return that rules-based logic can't deliver.

Critically, this is not a platform rip-and-replace. It runs alongside mParticle and Braze. It doesn't compete with the CDP decision — it builds on top of it.

What It Looks Like in Practice
Layer Role
mParticle Data routing — event collection, connector management, feeding Braze and analytics. Unchanged.
Einstein Studio Decisioning engine — trained on CMC's own client outcome data. Scores every client on churn risk, activity propensity, intervention priority. Updated continuously.
Data Cloud Unified client profile — connects CRM, trading activity, behavioural signals into a single view that feeds the decisioning model.
Braze / existing channels Activation — receives the decisioning output as a trigger signal. Same tools, better instructions.

The first use case — churn risk scoring on the active client book — can be scoped as a proof of concept with a defined success metric before any broader commitment is made.

Why This Is a Different Conversation
Different buyer

The CDP evaluation was owned by Marketing. Decisioning is a commercial and retention conversation — RMs, trading desk, client success. Different stakeholders, different value case.

Different BOM

No Mule. No full platform migration. A decisioning POC scoped to a single outcome — client churn — is a fraction of the commercial footprint of the CDP proposal.

Different risk profile

This doesn't touch the activation stack or require a migration. It sits on top of what's already running. The downside of a POC is a few weeks of effort. The upside is measurable.

Proven in financial services

The use case — client decisioning on a transactional book — is well-established in retail banking and wealth management. The patterns transfer directly to a trading platform context.

If This Is Worth a Conversation
  • 30-minute scoping call Walk through what a decisioning POC would look like for CMC specifically — what data, what outcome, what success looks like. No commitment, no proposal.
  • Define one use case Agree on a single, measurable outcome — client churn risk is the natural starting point. Scope the POC before any platform conversation begins.
  • Prove it, then decide A working model against a defined metric before any broader commitment. If it doesn't move the number, there's no case to make.
