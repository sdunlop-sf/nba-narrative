Minutes 0–3 · The Honest Open

Let's start with something uncomfortable.

Salesforce implementations have historically taken too long. We know it. You know it. Six-month programmes that become twelve-month programmes. Custom objects breeding custom objects. Consultancies billing by the quarter. The platform was powerful, but the path to value was slow.

That was the reality. And if we stand up here and pretend it wasn't, you shouldn't trust anything else we say.

But here's what's changed — and I mean structurally changed, not "we updated the marketing":

What slowed you down

  • Custom data models from scratch
  • Months of integration work
  • Separate tools for every channel
  • Rules logic maintained by specialists
  • Insight trapped in dashboards
  • "Phase 2" that never arrived

What's different now

  • Data Cloud — unified profiles, zero-copy
  • Industry data models out of the box
  • Agentforce — actions, not integrations
  • Headless APIs for programmatic access
  • AI reasoning at the platform level
  • Packages that deploy in days
The platform shifted from "build everything custom" to "configure, connect, deploy." The speed-to-value equation has fundamentally changed. And today I'm going to show you what that looks like for marketing decisioning in financial services.
This is your trust moment. You're not defending the past — you're naming it. Every practitioner in the room has lived the slow implementation. Acknowledge it, then show how the architecture moved. The credibility for the rest of the talk comes from this opening.

Minutes 8–11 · Two Ways In

Off the shelf. Or build your own. Same platform, your choice.

We're not asking you to pick one path. Some teams want turnkey. Some want total control. The platform supports both — and they're not mutually exclusive.

Mode A

Off the Shelf

Pre-built accelerators and packaged solutions. Configure, deploy, run. Production-grade decisioning without building from scratch.

  • Unlocked packages — deploy in days, not months
  • Pre-built flows — arbitration, scoring, feedback loops
  • Agentforce actions — 15+ structured tools, ready to go
  • Industry templates — financial services patterns baked in
  • Guided setup — conversational, not a 40-page guide

Best for: teams that want fast time-to-value with proven patterns.

Mode B

Build Your Own

Headless APIs. Composable primitives. Full programmatic control. Build your decisioning engine on the platform, your way.

  • Headless API — every capability accessible programmatically
  • Composable actions — assemble your own decisioning logic
  • BYOM — bring your own models alongside Einstein
  • Custom arbitration — your rules, your weighting, your policies
  • Platform as infrastructure — data + trust + execution layer

Best for: teams with strong engineering who want full architectural control.

Both modes share the same foundation: Data Cloud for the unified profile. Einstein Trust Layer for governance. Deterministic rails for the hot path. Agentic reasoning at the edges. The difference is how you get there — configured or coded.

This is "Make Marketing Great Again" for headless. The intelligence doesn't live in a UI you have to click through. It lives in APIs, actions, and composable primitives — accessible from anywhere, governed everywhere.
The two-modes framing defuses the "Salesforce is too opinionated" objection AND the "Salesforce is too complex" objection at the same time. Mode A is for the COO who wants results Thursday. Mode B is for the CTO who wants architectural control. Both are real. Both are valid. Let the room self-select.
Minutes 11–13 · Compliance & Flexibility

Deterministic rails. Agentic intelligence. Both.

In financial services, "the AI decided" is not an acceptable answer to a regulator. Every decision needs to be explainable, auditable, and reproducible. That means deterministic rails for the vast majority of decisions — with intelligence at the edges where it actually matters.

The Rails

Deterministic at Scale

99% of decisions are not ambiguous. A customer hits a threshold, eligible actions are clear, one wins on a weighted score. Sub-second. Auditable. Explainable. Cheap. No LLM in the hot path.

This is what well-built decisioning has looked like for twenty years — done right, with modern tooling. We don't claim this is revolutionary. We claim it's the correct modern implementation.

The Intelligence

Agentic at the Edges

The 1% that are ambiguous — conflicting signals, novel patterns, high-stakes decisions — escalate to an agent that reasons with full context. It recommends a treatment, drafts the content, writes rationale. A human approves.

This is the genuinely new part. And it only works because the rails handle everything else.

Neither is new on its own. Deterministic arbitration has existed for decades. LLM reasoning is everywhere now. The combination is new — and it's the only architecture that satisfies both the compliance team AND the marketing team.

The closed loop

Every decision writes an outcome signal back to the platform. Decision leads to action. Action leads to customer response. Response feeds the model. Model improves the next decision. Automated. Continuous. No manual retraining cycles.

Compliance officers want deterministic rails they can audit. Marketing wants intelligence that adapts. You don't pick one. You architect both — and you let each do what it's good at.
The honest framing matters here: "The deterministic core is good engineering, not a revolution. The agentic layer is the genuinely new part." Owning this distinction builds more credibility than overclaiming. The audience includes people who've built rules engines — they'll respect the honesty.
