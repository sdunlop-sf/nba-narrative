The Business Reality

Your customers are telling you
everything. You're not listening.

Not because the data isn't there. It is. The problem is the gap between knowing and doing — and it's costing more than most businesses realise.

<1%

Of customer data is ever acted on

The average enterprise collects more customer signal than ever before — transactions, behaviours, service interactions, loyalty activity. Almost none of it drives a decision. It sits in systems, unread, while the customer moves on.

5

Meaningful conversations a rep can have per day

Human capacity is the ceiling on relationship quality. With thousands of customers in the book, most get a generic email, a missed signal, or nothing at all. The best customers are often the quietest — and the most likely to leave without warning.

76%

Of customers expect personalisation — and notice when it's absent

Customers don't compare you to your direct competitors anymore. They compare you to every brand that's ever made them feel known. Generic outreach doesn't just underperform — it signals that you don't understand them.

Too late

When most businesses act on churn signals

By the time a customer calls to cancel, sends a complaint, or stops logging in — the decision is usually already made. The signals were there weeks earlier. The action never came. The window closed.

The problem isn't a lack of data or a lack of tools. It's the absence of a system that connects the signal to the action — automatically, intelligently, at the moment it matters — for every customer, not just the ones a rep happened to call this week.

The Complete Stack —
From Signal to Conversation

Before the detail, the map. Six layers — each one distinct, each one connected. NBA is the brain, but it needs the rest of the stack to deliver the full experience.

Layer 01
Data Cloud
Signal & Unification
The Signal
Unifies customer data across every touchpoint — transactions, behaviour, service history, loyalty — into a single profile. Detects events and segment changes that trigger the decisioning engine.
"Oliver has hit his account limit 3 times this month and his segment has changed to High Upgrade Intent."
Layer 02
NBA Engine
Einstein Studio + Flow
The Decision
Scores Oliver against every eligible NBA in the catalogue. Runs propensity × business value arbitration. Selects the winning action — which Promotion to offer, in which channel, for which reason.
"Winner: Limit Increase Offer — propensity 0.82 × value 90 = highest ranked. Channel: WhatsApp."
Layer 03
Promotions & Offers
Content Layer
The Content
The NBA engine selects the action type. Promotions & Offers builds what the customer actually receives — the incentive definition and its channel-specific expression.
Promotion

The incentive, channel-agnostic. "Increase your account limit by 50% — waive the first month fee." Defined once, reused across any channel.

Offer

The personalised expression for this customer in this channel. "Hi Oliver, here's your limit increase — one tap to activate, no fee for 30 days." Built at execution time.

Layer 04
Salesforce Personalization
Real-time ML
The Timing
Operates in real-time on digital surfaces — web, app, portal — where the NBA engine is too slow or too relationship-level. Its own ML recommends which Promotions to surface in the moment, based on in-session behaviour.
Personalization does

Real-time, in-session, behavioural. "Oliver just viewed the payroll add-on — surface the limit upgrade banner now."

NBA engine does

Relationship-level, signal-driven, cross-channel. "Oliver has shown 3 upgrade signals this month — adjudicate the best action."

They feed each other — Personalization generates behavioural signals that Data Cloud passes to the NBA engine. NBA decisions inform what Personalization surfaces next session.
Layer 05
Agentforce Marketing
Agentic Execution
The Conversation
Executes the winning Offer not as a static campaign, but as a two-way agentic conversation. The agent knows the context, handles replies and objections, escalates to a human rep only when the relationship demands it.
✉ Email
💬 SMS
📱 WhatsApp
🔔 Push
🌐 In-app
👤 Agent-assisted
"Hi Oliver, your account's been working hard lately — I'd like to offer you a limit increase. Interested?" → Oliver replies "tell me more" → agent continues the conversation, no human needed.
Layer 06
Einstein Studio
Feedback Loop
The Learning
Every outcome — accepted, declined, ignored — flows back through Data Cloud into Einstein Studio as a training signal. The model improves continuously. The more the engine runs, the better its decisions become.
"Oliver accepted → CampaignMember: Responded → Data Cloud → model retrains → propensity scores for similar customers improve → better decisions next run."

Master Flow
NBA Orchestration Engine
1
Load

Query the Active NBA Catalogue

Pull all NBA__c records where Status = Active and today falls within Valid_From / Valid_To. This is the candidate pool for this run.

No hardcoded actions — new NBAs are picked up automatically the moment they go Active.
2
Loop

For each NBA — Eligibility → Score → Gate

Three reusable subflows, called in sequence for every action in the catalogue. None of them know anything about specific NBAs — they process whatever record is passed in.

Subflow loop — runs per NBA
Subflow A
Eligibility Check

Is customer in the eligible Data Cloud segment? Frequency cap hit? Any active exclusions (complaint, churned)?

→
Subflow B
Score

Call Einstein Studio model at NBA.Model_ID__c. Get propensity score back for this customer + action.

→
Subflow C
Threshold Gate

Score ≥ NBA.Min_Propensity__c? Add to candidates with score × Business_Value. Below? Discard.

3–4
Decide

Arbitrate & Execute

Candidates collection is sorted and the winner is executed.

Step 3 — Arbitrate

Pick the winner

Sort candidates by propensity × Business_Value. Apply channel rules (one action per channel max). Top-ranked action wins. Ties broken by Priority__c.

Step 4 — Execute

Fire the action

Create CampaignMember against the NBA's Campaign. Surface to agent in-screen, trigger digital message, or queue for next rep interaction. Log decision for audit trail.

Feedback Loop
CampaignMember Outcome → Data Cloud → Einstein Studio
Customer responds → positive signal → model retrains → propensity improves → better decisions next run
Why not if/then statements

The catalogue is the configuration

Every eligibility rule, threshold, and value weight lives on the NBA record — not in the flow. Add a new NBA = create a record. Retire one = flip Status to Inactive. Zero code change required.

Scaling to production

Flow today, Apex at scale

The loop pattern works in Flow for moderate volumes. At scale, move the loop and scoring into Apex batch jobs — same architecture, same catalogue, just faster execution. Einstein Decision Management natively handles this at enterprise scale.
