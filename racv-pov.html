Loyalty as a Decisioning System for RACV

The Position RACV Is In

2.29 million members. $576 million in member benefits delivered last year. A leisure portfolio spanning eight resorts, two clubs, and a growing ecosystem of energy, home, travel, and finance products. RACV has something most organisations spend decades trying to build: a trusted, recurring relationship with the majority of Victorian households.

The question is not how to attract members. It's how to deepen what already exists. And right now, the depth of that relationship depends heavily on which division a member happens to interact with and how manually that interaction is managed.

The members who stay with RACV for life don't stay because of the points. They stay because RACV knows them. The opportunity is to make that true at scale: across every touchpoint, every business unit, and every partner in the ecosystem.
A Reference Worth Paying Attention To

CBA is pursuing a strategy of owning all customer transactions across the home: mortgage, everyday banking, insurance, energy, and wealth in a single relationship. The logic is straightforward: the more of a customer's life that flows through one trusted provider, the harder it becomes to leave and the more each interaction teaches you about the next one.

For RACV, the equivalent isn't financial. It's the moments that matter most across a member's life: getting home when the car breaks down, a weekend away with the family, insuring the house, switching to solar, planning a trip abroad. RACV already touches most of these. The gap is that each one happens in isolation. A separate interaction, a separate dataset, a separate opportunity that doesn't inform the next.

The loyalty program is the connective tissue. Not a points balance. A system that recognises a member across all of those moments, rewards the breadth of the relationship, and uses what it knows to determine what comes next.

Where the Opportunity Is

RACV Leisure is the underutilised asset in this picture. 196,000 members visited RACV resorts last year, but 177,000 visitors were non-members. Leisure is both a retention tool for existing members and an acquisition channel for new ones. It's also where the emotional connection to the RACV brand is strongest: a family holiday, a golf weekend, a spa day. Those experiences create loyalty that a roadside assistance renewal never can.

The partner network extends this further. A loyalty program that only rewards RACV products is inherently limited by RACV's own footprint. A partner ecosystem (fuel, retail, dining, entertainment) creates daily interaction with the loyalty program across moments RACV doesn't own. Every partner interaction is a signal. Every signal teaches the decisioning system something about what that member needs next.

This is the model Marriott built with Bonvoy. Not a hotel loyalty program, a lifestyle membership that earns and redeems across a global partner ecosystem, powered by unified data that makes every interaction more relevant than the last.

What Next Best Interaction Looks Like at Scale

RACV's Next Best Interaction program is already on the strategic roadmap. The proposed approach is to build the decisioning foundation on Data Cloud, connecting the member data that already exists across the Salesforce platform into a unified profile, and using that profile as the basis for intelligent interaction. The loyalty layer is what makes it signal-rich enough to work.

A member who has visited an RACV resort twice in the last year, holds a roadside and home insurance product, and recently enquired about solar is not a generic member. They are a high-value, multi-product member with a demonstrated interest in RACV's home and leisure proposition. The next best interaction for them is not a generic email. It's a personalised offer, at the right moment, determined by a decisioning engine that understands where they are in their relationship with RACV and what they're likely to value next.

The partner network feeds this system. Every transaction at a fuel partner, every dinner booked through a dining partner, every retail redemption is a signal that enriches the member profile and improves the next decision. The loyalty program becomes a data flywheel: the more members engage, the more the system learns, the better the next interaction becomes.

How the Platform Connects
Layer Role
Loyalty Management The programme layer. Manages member tiers, points currency, offers, promotions, and partner redemptions. Works across RACV's own products and the partner ecosystem from a single platform.
Data Cloud + Snowflake The unified member profile. Connects resort stays, insurance products, roadside events, partner transactions, and digital behaviour into one view of each member, updated continuously rather than in batch.
Einstein Studio The decisioning engine. Trained on RACV's own member outcome data, scoring each member on next best interaction: what to offer, when to offer it, through which channel. Learns from every outcome.
Marketing Cloud Next The delivery layer. Executes the next best interaction across email, SMS, app, and web. Already in implementation. The decisioning layer determines what fires and when.
Agentforce The service layer. Already live in the contact centre. Surfaces member context and next best action to agents in real time, closing the loop between digital decisioning and human interaction.

This is not a greenfield build. The Salesforce foundation is already in place. The loyalty and decisioning layers sit on top of infrastructure RACV is actively investing in — they accelerate the roadmap rather than adding to it.

A Suggested Starting Point
  • Leisure loyalty as the first use case Define what the loyalty proposition looks like for RACV Leisure specifically: how resort stays, club memberships, and partner offers are rewarded, and what the next best interaction looks like for a high-value leisure member.
  • Partner ecosystem scoping Identify the partner categories that create daily frequency: fuel, dining, retail, entertainment. Define the data model that connects partner transactions back to the member profile and feeds the decisioning engine.
  • Connect to the Data 360 roadmap Map the loyalty data model to the Snowflake and Data Cloud implementation already underway. The Next Best Interaction program is most powerful when loyalty signals are part of the member profile it runs on.
  • Vision session with the right stakeholders Bring the Leisure, Loyalty, and Data leadership together. The decisioning and technology conversation is more compelling when it starts with the member experience RACV wants to own.
