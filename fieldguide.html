Words to listen for

If you hear any of these, note it and act on it. The opportunity is usually bigger than it first appears.

Three questions to ask
1
"What are you using AI for today — internally or customer-facing?"
Uncovers LLM usage, governance gaps, and where decisions are being made without IT sign-off. Most customers are doing more than they admit.
2
"How are you thinking about personalisation or next best action at scale?"
Places them on the maturity model. "We're not there yet" is an opening. "We tried Pega" is a hot lead.
3
"What's your biggest concern about moving faster on AI?"
Surfaces the real blocker — usually data security, compliance, or lack of expertise. This is exactly what the Salesforce platform solves.

Five-beat narrative
1
The market moment
Claude and ChatGPT are already in the building. Marketers, analysts, ops teams are using them today — often without IT knowing. The risk isn't AI adoption. It's ungoverned AI adoption. This is a now conversation, not a future one.
2
The problem with going direct
You can wire Claude or GPT straight into your stack via API. But you own the risk — no audit trail, customer data in a model you don't control, no human-in-the-loop. Compliance will shut it down the moment they find out. And they will find out.
3
The Salesforce answer
Bring your own model. Salesforce becomes the governance wrapper. Data stays in your org. Agentforce is the front end — your CRM, your channels, your rules. The LLM is the engine. Salesforce is the chassis. Anthropic's interests are aligned here — enterprise adoption through a governed platform is their path too.
4
The Pega contrast
Every enterprise has a graveyard of Pega, Unica, or SAS implementations — expensive, multi-year, never finished. Same ambition. AI-native competitors like MoEngage are already doing this faster and cheaper. The question isn't whether your customers move. It's whether Salesforce is the platform they move on.
5
The business case closer
This is a digital labour story. The work that takes 80 people today can be done with 5. Personalised decisioning at scale, in real time, without a data science team. That's the CFO conversation. Lead with outcomes, not architecture.
Common objections
?
"We're already talking to Anthropic / OpenAI directly."
Good — they're doing the market education for us. The question isn't which LLM, it's how they govern it, connect it to their CRM, and get it into the hands of the business safely. That's the gap we fill.
?
"Our data can't go into an AI model."
Correct — and it doesn't have to. Data stays in the Salesforce org. The LLM reasons on what it's given, not on raw customer data. We can show exactly how the masking and data flow works in a sandbox with anonymised data.
?
"We don't have the data science resources to build this."
That's the point. This doesn't require a data science team. Einstein Studio, Data Cloud, and the decisioning layer are built to be operated by the business — not by engineers. We can pair with SF services to get the first use case live fast.
